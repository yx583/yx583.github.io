Music

Guzheng

A traditional Chinese plucked zither instrument with a history that dates back more than 2,500 years.

It has a long and flat soundbox, with a set of 21 or more strings stretched over individual movable bridges. The player plucks the strings with one hand, while using the other hand to press or bend the strings to create vibrato or pitch variations.

The instrument has gained popularity around the world and is recognized as one of the most important cultural symbols of China.
